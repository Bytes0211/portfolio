Transforming Distributions

Transformation is the process of adding, subtracting some constant value from the measures of central tendency of a distribution. Below you can see the impact (shifting) of adding a constant:

Shifting A Distribution



  • lets say k = 1 standard deviation
  • The red distribution is a standard normal distribution
  • A shift to the right denotes a positive shift of 1 std dev as depicted with the blue distribution
  • A negative distribution would be a shift to the left 1 std dev
  • $\displaystyle \mu_x = 0$
  • $\displaystyle \mu_y = 1$
  • The range and the IQR stay the same because the distribution is not stretched or shrunk
  • The range from the start of the distribution to the end of the distribution is the same so range did not change
  • The spread around the median did not change so the IQR is the same
  • We see that $\displaystyle \sigma_x = sigma_y$

Scaling A Distribution

Lets say k = 2
We multiply the dataset X by k what happens?


When we multiply X by k, mean, median, mode, range, and IQR are all doubled. This results in the distribution being doubled due to the multiplication of X by k. Keep in mind the area of the distribution cannot change. So the if the length of the distribution doubles, the height of the distribution must be halved. In summary:

  • the width of the distribution is 2 X
  • mean, median, mode, range, and IQR are all multiplied by 2
  • The standard deviation is scaled by k you as well by multiplying by 2
